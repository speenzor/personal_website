My Past

I was born and raised in beautiful southern California. In 2005 I was admitted to the highly selective and prestigious St. Margaret’s Episcopal School for the 8th grade and high school. After graduating from St. Margaret’s I attended the well-known and also very prestigious Whitman College in Walla Walla, WA to study economics and business. There I learned the inner workings of economics and accounting that would form the foundation of my knowledge later in life. At Whitman I was also a highly successful cross country and track athlete, leading my team from 7th place in the conference in 2012 to 3rd place in 2013. My time as a student-athlete honed my skills in leadership, teamwork, and communication. In 2014 I graduated and was off into the real world to continue building valuable skills.

After graduation I worked a wide array of jobs to build skills in many different fields. I worked at a call center, worked at a running shoe store, drove for Uber, worked at a cleaning company, worked for a real estate developer, went door to door for a presidential campaign, worked at a commercial real estate mortgage broker, and worked an unpaid internship at a private equity firm. I currently work at a community bank underwriting and originating business loans and relationships.

My Present

I decided to get into finance after noticing that in the commercial real estate industry you start at the bottom as a broker and then eventually work up to being an equity owner by honing your skills, relationships, and knowledge. As you do your job you become better at your next job.

I recently leveraged my budding skills in business development and business analysis to join Amerigo Capital in Irvine, as a full-time unpaid intern, in order to gain more relevant experience while looking for the next appropriate opportunity. Amerigo Capital is a new private equity firm and we are close to closing Amerigo’s first-ever deal, selling a boutique, brand-name fragrance company to a strategic acquirer. We are also about to purchase a well positioned specialty pharmaceutical company that will provide us with a strategic platform in the lucrative and growing specialty pharmaceutical industry.

This lead me to think…what is the real estate broker’s equivalent in business? And conclude that banking would be a good way to build relevant skills and see what’s out there.

My Future

Discipline, persistence, frugality, focus, goal-orientation, attention to detail, strict fasting and dieting, how to learn quickly, communication. I practice these skills by fasting and dieting, running, reading a ton, networking, writing.

I like to contribute my energy to small, non-bureaucratic teams that will allow me to work with a variety of people and challenges, and learn quickly.
