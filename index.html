My Past

Born and raised in southern California. Went to a posh private high school. Went to an unknown private college. Graduated in 2014. Worked six jobs spanning politics, janitorial services, real estate, finance, Uber driver, retail, and freelance.

Since graduating from Whitman College in 2014 with a degree in economics I have worked at several diverse jobs. I started out working for a real estate development firm called StoneCreek Company doing due diligence work for several projects, including the acquisition of a retail property that was successfully repositioned to retail and medical office. After I gained valuable knowledge of the real estate industry and developed great attention to detail I desired to move into the financial side of real estate and obtained a position at Lucent Capital, a highly respected commercial real estate mortgage broker in Beverly Hills.

In 2016, while working at Lucent Capital, I personally paid for and completed the Investment Banking institute course with investment banker and friend, Chuck Bush, of Great Road Capital. This course takes place over four weekends and it is a significant financial and time commitment that honed my skills in financial modeling, including LBO and merger models.

My Present

I read How to Create a Mind, by Ray Kurzweil, which blew my mind and forever altered the course of history. Now an experienced banker building his skills in software creation. Learning: JavaScript, Python, HTML, CSS, Git/GitHub, React.JS, Bootstrap.

I recently leveraged my budding skills in business development and business analysis to join Amerigo Capital in Irvine, as a full-time unpaid intern, in order to gain more relevant experience while looking for the next appropriate opportunity. Amerigo Capital is a new private equity firm and we are close to closing Amerigo’s first-ever deal, selling a boutique, brand-name fragrance company to a strategic acquirer. We are also about to purchase a well positioned specialty pharmaceutical company that will provide us with a strategic platform in the lucrative and growing specialty pharmaceutical industry.

At Whitman I was an active member of the Whitman Investment Company, a student-run club that met bi-weekly to discuss and implement investments for a portfolio worth over $700,000. I regularly presented research reports on businesses such as Apple, Tesla, Qualcomm, Wal-Mart, Rio Tinto and others. I was elected CFO and reported quarterly financial results of the portfolio. I successfully recommended the group invest in Apple in 2013 leading to over 80% appreciation in stock value.

As a member of Lion’s Heart I held several positions within my group, including President, lead my group to many service projects, and was awarded The President’s Volunteer Service Award. Lion’s Heart is now run by my Mother and her amazing team.
